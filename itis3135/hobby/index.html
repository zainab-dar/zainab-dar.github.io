Making Cakes ~ What

Welcome to My Cake Making Journey

Over the last year, I've gotten into baking and decorating cakes, specifically tres leches cakes! I love experimenting with different cake themes, testing icing textures, and creating my own homemade fillings.

Signature Styles

  • Fresh Homemade Strawberry Jam Filling
  • Fluffy, Creamy Whipped Icing
  • Multi-layer Tres Leches Cake

What Makes Tres Leches Special

Tres leches cake is a traditional Latin American dessert soaked in three kinds of milk: evaporated milk, condensed milk, and heavy cream. The result is an incredibly moist and rich cake that melts in your mouth. I love how versatile it is - you can add different flavors and toppings to create unique variations for every occasion.

Making Cakes ~ Who

Who Am I

I'm Zainab! I started cake decorating after watching several TikTok videos of cake decorators making the most intricate and yummy cakes and being inspired to do the same.

Who do I bake for

I bake for family events and special occasions. I'm still working on getting smoother bases and perfecting my technique, but I hope to eventually start a small side business making and selling cakes for other people.

My Inspiration

My biggest inspiration comes from professional bakers on social media. I eventually decided to give it a shot myself and bake a cake for a small home gathering. I'm still constantly learning new techniques and challenging myself with more complex designs and growing every single day.

Making Cakes ~ When

When do I bake

I bake all year round; each season inspires new flavors and decorations. Most recently, for my sister's wedding, I got to make several cakes for her events which was incredibly special.

Seasonal Specialties

Season Favorite Flavors
Spring Strawberry, Lemon, Vanilla
Summer Mango, Peach, tres leches
Fall Pumpkin Spice, tres leches
Winter Chocolate, tres leches, biscoff

Best Times to Bake

I typically bake on weekends when I have more time to focus on the intricate details. For events, I usually start baking 2-3 days in advance to allow time for proper assembly and decoration. The night before an event is when I do the final touches and transport preparation.

Making Cakes ~ Where

Where do I bake

I typically bake in my home kitchen. I usually make one cake at a time so am not in need of a bigger space. The biggest cake I've baked was a seven-layer two-tier cake that I had to store as two separate cakes until it was close to the event time and I could stack them.

My Baking Setup

My kitchen has a dedicated corner for cake decorating with all my tools organized and ready to go. I have a stand mixer, multiple cake pans in different sizes, a turntable for decorating, and various piping tips and spatulas.

Where I Source Ingredients

I get most of my baking supplies from local grocery stores, but for specialty items like unique sprinkles, food coloring, and cake decorating tools, I shop at hobby lobby or order online. I solely use fresh fruits and make my fillings and whip from scratch which really makes a difference in the flavor of my fillings and toppings.

Making Cakes ~ How

Techniques I Use

  • Crumb coat and chilled layering
  • Soaking cake in tres leches mix overnight
  • Support dowel methods for tiered cakes

My Process Step-by-Step

First, I bake the cake layers and let them cool completely. Then I level each layer and soak them with the tres leches mixture. After refrigerating overnight, I apply a crumb coat of whipped icing and chill again. Finally, I add the final layer of frosting, pipe decorations, and stack the layers with fresh filligns and other toppings. Each step requires patience but the end results are worth it.

Essential Tools

The most important tools for making my cakes are my offset spatula for smooth frosting, piping bags with various tips for decorative work, a cake turntable for even coating, and a good quality stand mixer, i truly couldn't manage having to hand whip everything. I also can't live without my cake leveler, bench scraper, and dowel rods for multi-tiered cakes.

Making Cakes ~ Why

Why I Bake

Baking is a form of creative therapy for me. It's both technical and artistic, which makes it so rewarding. It's a perfect mix of patience, skill, and creativity that I find very enjoyable.

Sharing With Others

Making cakes creates small moments of joy. Seeing friends and family enjoy what I made and look forward to having my cake at any occasion is especially rewarding.

Future Goals

My long-term goal is to turn this hobby into a small side business. I want to continue perfecting my craft, experimenting with new flavors and designs, and eventually take custom orders for special events. Beyond that, I'd love to teach cake decorating workshops to share my passion with others in my community.
